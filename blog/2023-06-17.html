As part of the Dev academy curriculum I'm working on my interpersonal and intrapersonal skills. The first step in this process is to identify my core values along with my strengths and weaknesses. I feel that I've done quite a bit of this type of learning in the past through various experiences. I've had to think about these things in sporting, professinal and hobbiest settings. It's good to reflect on my values every so often because they are likely to change as I get older and my prioreties change. Below are some examples of my identity, values, strengths and weaknesses with accompying examples.

  1. Ethical decision

    I initially struggled to think of an ethical decision that I had to make. Upon some research into ethical decisions I realised that my decision to change to a vegetarian diet could be considerd an ethical decision. I've been following a vegetarian diet for over 3 years now (with the exception of when Cyclone Gabrielle hit our region). I decided to become a vegetarian as scientific research suggests that has a lower impact on our environment. Most sources of meat require a vast amount of land and water to produce food compared to plant foods. The case for animal cruelty does have a small impact on my decision making but the environment was the main factor in my decision. It hasn't always been easy following the diet as I am the only one in my household. I still believe in my decision however and do not regret it.
